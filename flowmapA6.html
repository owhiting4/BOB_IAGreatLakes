Assignment 6: Flow Mapping on FlowMapper

This assignment uses flowmapper.org to map family tree movement across the United States from 1887-1924

Spatial Patterns

Sending/Recieving Flows

The places that receive the most flows are the western states, along with Florida, especially California and the Pacific northwest. The states that send the most flows are in the South and Midwest, particularly around Tenesse, Mississippi, and Alabama.

General Direction of Flows

The general direction is from east to west, which make sense because the western states were much newer at the time that this data represents. There are more flows between adjacent states, which can be seen in states starting at New York and branching through each state going west, so from New York, to Pennsylvania, to Ohio, to Indiana, to Illinois, and so on all the way to Texas. The exceptions are California and Texas, who have flows spanning multiple states.

Flow Patterns in Relation to Location Characteristics

We can see by the diverging color scheme what states have more flows out of the state and which states have more flows coming in. The node provides a representation of the volume of flows. Oklahoma also has a positive net flow, but a high valued node, meaning there are a lot of people moving in and out of Oklahoma, but more people are coming than leaving.

Here in this map, the base polygon symbology represents the overall direction of movement throughout all states(east to west). The nodes show the volume of traffic (flows coming both in and out) of each state. The flows themselves show specific movement and direction between each state.
